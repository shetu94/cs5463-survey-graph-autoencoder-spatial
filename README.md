## Survey Title  
Survey on Graph Convolutional Autoencoders for Spatial Shape Representation and Analysis  

## Category  
Approximate Similarity Search over Polygonal/Spatial Datasets  

---

## Problem Statement  

Spatial shape representation is a fundamental challenge in geographic information systems (GIS), urban computing, and spatial data analysis. Traditional methods for encoding and comparing geometric shapes such as buildings, parcels, and polygonal regions often rely on handcrafted feature extraction techniques that fail to capture rich structural and topological relationships. As geospatial datasets grow in size and complexity, scalable and learnable approaches are required to encode spatial shapes into compact and semantically meaningful representations.

---

## Scope of the Survey  

This survey focuses on Graph Convolutional Autoencoders (GCAEs) as a unified framework for spatial shape representation, similarity search, and cognition. Specifically, it will cover:  
1. Foundational work on autoencoders and Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs)  
2. Applications of GCAEs to polygonal and spatial datasets  
3. Approximate similarity search over spatial embeddings  
4. Evaluation benchmarks and real-world applications  

The goal is to synthesize existing literature, categorize current techniques, and identify open research challenges.

---

## Key Topics to be Covered  

- Graph-based shape representation and encoding  
- Convolutional autoencoders for geometric learning  
- Approximate nearest neighbor search over spatial embeddings  
- Spatial cognition and map generalization using deep learning  
- Benchmark datasets and evaluation metrics
