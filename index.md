## Survey Title  
Survey on Graph Convolutional Autoencoders for Spatial Shape Representation and Analysis  

## Category  
Approximate Similarity Search over Polygonal/Spatial Datasets  

---

## Problem Statement  

Spatial shape representation is a fundamental challenge in geographic information systems (GIS), urban computing, and spatial data analysis. Traditional methods for encoding and comparing geometric shapes such as buildings, parcels, and polygonal regions often rely on handcrafted feature extraction techniques that fail to capture the rich structural and topological relationships inherent in spatial data. As the volume and complexity of geospatial datasets continue to grow, there is an increasing demand for scalable, learnable approaches that can automatically encode spatial shapes into compact, semantically meaningful representations.

---

## Scope of the Survey  

This survey focuses on Graph Convolutional Autoencoders (GCAEs) as a unified framework for spatial shape representation, similarity search, and cognition. Specifically, the survey will cover: (i) foundational work on autoencoders and graph convolutional networks (GCNs) for geometric and spatial data; (ii) recent advances in applying GCAEs to polygonal and spatial datasets including building footprints, map shapes, and geospatial objects; (iii) techniques for approximate similarity search over encoded spatial representations; and (iv) evaluation benchmarks and real-world applications in cartographic generalization, urban analysis, and spatial retrieval systems. The survey aims to synthesize existing literature, identify open research challenges, and highlight promising future directions in this interdisciplinary domain.


---

## Key Topics to be Covered  

The survey will cover the following key areas: graph-based shape representation and encoding; convolutional autoencoders for geometric learning; approximate nearest-neighbor and similarity search over spatial embeddings; spatial cognition and map generalization using deep learning; and benchmark datasets and evaluation metrics for spatial shape analysis.
